Students receive a single word or theme. From that seed, they have one week to build anything they can imagine — then defend it live on event day.

OpenCanvas is TechQuest's most unrestricted event. No predefined tools, no required outputs, no wrong answers. A student given "A Tree" might build a family history explorer, a breathing ecosystem simulation, a puzzle game, or an interactive art piece. Every interpretation is valid.

The event has two stages: a week-long build where participants craft their creation in their own time, and a live event day where they pitch their work and face a surprise challenge — testing their ability to think, adapt, and communicate under real pressure.

Phase 02 · Live Day  —  ~105 Minutes
Total Duration~105 minutes
Part A — The Pitch3-min demo + 2-min judge Q&A

JudgingPanel of 3 judges, blind scoring

Part A — The Pitch: You walk up, show your project, and talk the judges through what you built and why. You have 3 minutes — show it off, explain your idea, and tell them what makes it yours. Then the judges ask you two questions. Don't worry, there are no trick questions — they just want to understand your thinking.

Part B — The Mood Shift: This is the fun part. On the day, you'll be handed a theme — a single word or short phrase, like "Haunted" or "Festive" or "Underwater." Every team gets a different one. You then have 90 minutes to go back into your project and make it feel like that theme. Change the colours, mess with the fonts, add a weird animation, hide something creepy in a corner — anything goes as long as the mood comes through. You're not rebuilding from scratch, you're transforming what you already made.

Example Moods:  "Haunted"  ·  "Underwater"  ·  "Broken & Glitchy"  ·  "Festive"  ·  "Ancient"  ·  "Dreamy"  ·  "Danger Zone"  ·  "Tiny & Cute"

✦   You won't know your mood until the day itself. How you prepare your project before that — and how you manage what's inside it — is completely up to you.

Phase 01 · The Build
50 pts

Creativity & Originality

20

How unique and imaginative is the interpretation of the prompt? Rewards bold, unconventional ideas over safe, predictable ones. The more surprising the angle, the higher the score.

Technical Execution & Effort

15

Is the project well built for the participant's age and skill level? Judges assess whether it works reliably, the quality of craft, and how much effort is visible in the final product — not just complexity.

Depth & Completeness

10

Does the project feel complete and thought through? Multiple layers, features, or details that show sustained focus over the week will score higher than a rushed or surface-level build.

Relevance to Prompt

5

Does the project connect meaningfully to the given topic? Abstract or unexpected interpretations are perfectly valid as long as the participant can explain the link.

Phase 02 · Live Day
50 pts

The Pitch — Presentation & Clarity

15

How clearly and confidently did you present your project? Judges want to see that you understand what you built and can explain it simply. Walking them through your idea, showing it working, and answering their questions calmly will score well here. You don't need to be perfect — just genuine.

Mood Shift — How Well the Mood Comes Through

20

Does your project actually feel like the theme you were given? Judges will look at the colours, the atmosphere, what you added or changed, and whether the overall experience matches the mood. A surface-level change — like just switching the background to black for "Haunted" — won't score as well as something that really feels the part.

Mood Shift — Creativity & Surprise

15

Did you go beyond the obvious? Hidden easter eggs, unexpected animations, quirky details that only show up if you look closely — these are what judges remember. If someone exploring your "Haunted" supermarket finds a ghost silently walking through the dairy aisle, that's a 15. Surprise the judges.

The Best Project Takes the Stage
The participant or team with the highest combined score will have their project featured live at TechQuest's closing ceremony — demoed in front of the entire school, teachers, and guests.
Eligibility: All participants with a submitted project qualify automatically.
Selection: Highest grand total score across both phases wins the spotlight.
Tiebreaker: In case of a tie, the Phase 01 Creativity score decides.
On the day: The winner is announced at the closing ceremony and given 3–5 minutes to present their creation to the full audience. Their name is displayed on the event board.

A few things to know before you start building.

Open ToClasses 5 – 12
Team SizeSolo or teams of 2 – 3
ToolsAnything — no restrictions
SubmissionLink or file before event day
Code ReviewNot checked — fully yours

Your code is your own. Judges evaluate what they can see and experience in the final output — not what is inside your files. How you write, structure, or organise your code is entirely up to you. What you put in it, what you leave out, and how you manage it is your business.

No restrictions on tools or approach. You can use any programming language, framework, design tool, no-code platform, or combination of anything. If it helps you build something great, use it. There is no "right" way to participate.

On event day, bring your best. Make sure your project is accessible and working before you arrive. You'll be presenting it live to judges, so test it in advance. Broken demos won't score well — even if the idea is great.

✦   The Mood Shift theme is different for every team and is only revealed on the day. You won't know it in advance — but how you handle that is entirely your call. Use the build week however you like.
