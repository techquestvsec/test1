Public Speaking

Debate

Parliamentary-style debate — research your stance, deliver structured speeches, and handle a live crisis round.

Format: Parliamentary Debate
Classes: 9–12
Mode: Individual
Stance: For / Against
Overview

An agenda is shared one week before the event. Participants must submit their stance (For or Against) at least 2 days prior — assigned first-come-first-served so both sides have equal representation.

A research / position paper must be submitted before the event containing: opening & closing speeches, data pointers, a clear stance, and referenced facts.

Session Structure
Session 1
Opening Speeches
Each participant delivers an opening speech (60–90 seconds). Cross-questioning follows if time permits.
Session 2
Formal Debate
Individual speaker time: 2–2.5 minutes. Commentary and cross-questioning are open to all participants and the host. Speakers may refer to scripts for subtopics.
Session 3
Crisis Round
A hypothetical crisis is declared by the judge. Same-stance participants team up to respond. A handwritten resolution paper must be submitted (your stance on the crisis and how you handled it).
Session 4
Closing Speeches
Each participant delivers a 1.5-minute closing speech. No cross-questioning in this session.
Rules
  • Strictly formal language only — no informal speech.
  • No targeting of any individual, caste, religion, gender, or community.
  • Violations result in debarment for the remainder of the session.
  • Improvisation is allowed in opening and closing speeches.
  • Neutrality is not accepted — you must take a clear stance.
